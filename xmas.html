The Advent calendar

The Advent calendar first originated in Germany in the 19th century. It was used by the Lutherans to count the days from the first of December to the 24th, which is Christmas in Germany, and has since spread outside of religion as well. Designs of Advent calendars vary vastly, with some still having religious themes whilst others follow the season's theme whilst its contents range from chocolate to toys to cosmetic items.
